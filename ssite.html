The greatest gift to life on Earth is trees. The idea that trees behave as carbon sinks by storing carbon dioxide in their roots, woods, and leaves has already been discussed, but I want to emphasise it. This carbon is not as abundant in our climate, which has the potential to destroy it. A tree can generate wind.

Long Speech on Tree Plantation
The activity of planting trees is referred to as afforestation or reforestation in silviculture. In this technique, seedlings are planted in areas where the forest has suffered damage from a fire, disease, harvesting, or other human activities. As a result of the alarming rate at which pollution is growing, numerous nations are developing various measures to combat the problem.
One of the most significant of these strategies is the planting of trees. Numerous reforestation businesses have also been established, and they are in charge of planting trees in the areas that have lost their forests. By removing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen into the atmosphere, trees improve the air quality for all living things.
Advantages of Afforestation
Carbon Dioxide and oxygen are exchanged in a whole cycle in plants. Thus, more tree planting would aid in lowering the atmospheric concentration of carbon dioxide. The more trees planted, the cleaner the air will be. The air would be pure and free of toxins if there were sufficient oxygen and reduced carbon dioxide. Plants can also naturally filter contaminated water. Thus, increasing tree cover would improve not only the air quality but also the water. The quantity of plants has decreased, which has caused the temperature to change regularly. Plants can sustain atmospheric temperatures. Increasing the number of plants would help cool down hotter areas. Numerous wild animals live in trees and other plants. Many birds construct their nests on trees. Plants support wildlife. Increased tree planting will benefit all biodiversity in addition to humans. The ecosystem is immediately improved by planting trees. Planting trees can stop soil erosion. Additionally, they absorb various contaminants, making the land, water, and air cleaner.
Deforestation
Deforestation is the deliberate destruction of forests. Typically, many trees are taken down to make more room for people. The rapid increase in population is the cause of this. People need more land to construct dwellings or provide space for expanding crop production. Additionally, forests are being cleared to produce more wood, which is utilised as a fuel and a building material. If we keep destroying our ecosystem at our current rate, soon, no trees will be left on Earth. So, in order to stop nature's destruction, mankind must plant more trees. Story of My School Recently my school has started a tree plantation campaign around the city. We have to go to different places in the city and plant trees in those places where there is none. I had lots of trees with me, and I planted almost 100 trees around colonies, colleges, schools and outside some corporate buildings. I was delighted throughout the campaign. Without trees, none of us would survive. Thus we need to plant trees and make our mother earth greener. We know how crucial plants and trees are to our continued existence and health. But how many of us plant trees regularly? If you aren't already, now is the moment to start taking environmental improvement seriously and making whatever small contributions you can.
